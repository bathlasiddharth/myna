# People Management — Features

**Scope:** Person files, observations, contributions, feedback, recognition, 1:1 analysis, engagement signals, performance narratives.

---

## Features

> **Moved to writing-and-drafts.md:** Recognition Distribution — drafting recognition messages for multiple channels is a writing/output concern, not data management. See "Recognition Draft" in that domain.

### Person File Management

One-line summary: Each person in the registry gets a dedicated file that accumulates everything Myna knows about them.

- Each person in the registry has a file under `myna/People/{person-name}.md`
- File sections: overview (role, team, relationship tier), communication preferences, observations timeline, recognition log, personal notes, links to meeting files
- Personal notes section for non-work details: hobbies, family, milestones
- "Note about Sarah: training for a marathon" → adds to personal notes in her person file
- Milestones (work anniversaries, birthdays) surfaced in daily note when upcoming — configurable in workspace config (`milestones_enabled`)
- Created automatically when a person is added to the registry

### Observations & Feedback Logging

One-line summary: Log observations about anyone — strengths, growth areas, contributions, feedback given — with two-path write safety.

- Each observation includes: type (strength / growth area / contribution), date, context, area, coaching-tone suggestion for how to deliver
- Three write paths:
  - **User-typed** ("observation about Sarah: great escalation handling") → direct write with `[User]` tag
  - **Agent-extracted, high confidence** (explicit from source — e.g., email clearly says "Sarah did a great job on the incident") → direct write tagged `[Auto]` with source reference. Auditable but doesn't clog the review queue.
  - **Agent-extracted, uncertain** (inferred from context — e.g., agent thinks a comment might be praise but isn't sure) → review queue with inferred fields marked
- Before writing any observation, the agent reads the person file's existing observations to avoid duplicating something already logged (by the user or by a previous processing run)
- Feedback logging: same three paths — explicit feedback in an email writes directly with `[Auto]`, ambiguous signals go to review queue
- Pending feedback summary at top of person file: undelivered observations with talking points
- Feeds into: 1:1 prep, performance narratives, recognition, self-tracking (feedback you gave is also a contribution)

### Recognition Tracking

One-line summary: Track accomplishments and recognition for team members in their person files.

- Each recognition entry: date, what they did, source/context, category
- Three write paths (same pattern as observations):
  - **User-typed** → direct write with `[User]` tag
  - **Agent-extracted, high confidence** (explicit recognition in source) → direct write tagged `[Auto]`
  - **Agent-extracted, uncertain** (might be politeness, not genuine recognition) → review queue
- Feeds into: recognition drafts (writing-and-drafts domain), performance narratives, person briefings

### Person Briefing

One-line summary: "Brief me on [person]" gives instant context about anyone before a meeting or conversation.

- Pulls from person file, project files, meeting history, task lists:
  - Role, team, audience tier
  - Projects they're on and current status
  - Recent interactions — last meetings, recent email/Slack exchanges in vault
  - Open items between you — tasks you delegated to them, things they're waiting on from you
  - Pending feedback — undelivered observations with talking points
  - Personal notes — hobbies, milestones, things they've mentioned
  - Last 1:1 date and carry-forward items
  - Stakeholder mentions (for cross-team contacts, PMs, VPs): factual list of where this person appears in your project data — what they said in meetings (from your notes), email exchanges in the vault, timeline entries that mention them. Presented as raw mentions with dates, NOT as interpreted stance or position. The user connects the dots.
- Works for anyone: directs, peers, manager, cross-team contacts, VPs
- Output shown inline

### 1:1 Pattern Analysis

One-line summary: Surfaces factual patterns in your 1:1 notes — action item follow-through, recurring topics, carry-forward trends — without judging relationship quality.

- "Analyze my 1:1s with Sarah" → reviews recent sessions and reports FACTS:
  - Action item follow-through: X of Y action items from last session were addressed. Lists the ones that weren't.
  - Recurring unresolved topics: topics that appeared in 3+ sessions without resolution. Lists them with dates.
  - Carry-forward rate: what percentage of topics carry forward to the next session.
  - Topic source balance: how many topics were added by you vs. carried from previous vs. generated by prep.
- Output shown inline with specific examples from notes

### Feedback Gap Detection

One-line summary: Configurable feedback cycle per person — when the gap exceeds threshold, it automatically surfaces as a topic in 1:1 prep.

- Configured in workspace config: `feedback_cycle_days` (default: 30 days). Can also be overridden per person in registry if some people need different cadences.
- When the gap between now and last logged feedback exceeds the threshold, the 1:1 prep for that person automatically includes: "No feedback logged in X days — consider discussing growth areas or recent work"
- Also surfaced in Team Health Overview as a flag per person
- Not a standalone feature the user has to remember to ask for — it's woven into the prep flow. The user sees it when they need it (before the 1:1), not as a separate report they have to pull.

### Performance Narrative

One-line summary: Generates a performance narrative for a team member's review cycle from accumulated data.

- "Generate performance narrative for Sarah, last 6 months" → compiles from: observations log, recognition entries, contributions, meeting notes, project timeline mentions
- Default time period: last 3 months (configurable per request)
- Output reads as a genuine professional narrative — no AI tells, no hedging, no formulaic structure
- Saved as draft in `Drafts/` for user editing before use in review cycle
- Highlights `[Inferred]` entries in the source data so the user knows which data points to double-check before finalizing the narrative
- **Review calibration mode:** "review my narratives" — after drafting narratives for multiple directs, analyzes the set for consistency. Checks: is evidence depth proportional across people? Are similar contributions described with comparable strength? Are there unconscious patterns (longer narratives for some, more hedging language for others)? Flags discrepancies for the manager to address. This doesn't auto-correct — it surfaces the data so you can make fairer assessments.

### Team Health Overview

One-line summary: "How is my team doing?" — a single view showing all your directs' status, workload, and relationship health at a glance.

- Shows for each direct report (all factual, zero inference):
  - Current projects and workload (number of open tasks, overdue items)
  - Last 1:1 date and days until next one
  - Outstanding delegations (overdue / approaching deadline)
  - Last feedback date and feedback gap flag
  - Recent contributions (last 2 weeks)
  - Attention gaps (from Attention Gap Detection — e.g., "no observations logged in 45 days")
- Output as a structured table/summary, queryable on demand
- Surfaced as a section in the Unified Dashboard (daily-workflow) for ongoing visibility
- Separate from individual person briefings — this is the portfolio view, not the deep dive
- **Why this is needed:** A manager with 6-10 directs can't keep everything in their head. Without this, you'd open 8 person files to answer "who needs my attention?" This is the view that prevents things from falling through the cracks — the direct who hasn't had a 1:1 in 3 weeks, the one with 4 overdue delegations, the one you haven't given feedback to in 2 months. It surfaces the quiet problems, not just the loud ones.

### Team Health Tracking

One-line summary: Longitudinal team health — track trends across retros, 1:1s, and team metrics over time in a dedicated team file.

- **Team file** (`myna/Team/{team-name}.md`) — parallel to project/person files. Contains:
  - Team overview (members, structure)
  - Timeline of team-level observations: retro themes, cross-1:1 patterns, process changes, hiring/departures
  - Each entry uses standard date + source format and provenance markers
- **Populated from multiple sources:**
  - Retro processing → recurring themes, action items, what's working/not working
  - Cross-1:1 pattern detection → when the same concern appears in multiple 1:1s ("3 people mentioned unclear ownership this month"), agent flags it as a team-level observation with `[Inferred]` tag
  - Manual observations → "team note: morale is up after the launch" with `[User]` tag
- **Snapshot log for trend tracking:** weekly summary (or Team Health Overview) appends a dated snapshot of key metrics — delegation overdue count, feedback gap count, attention gap count per person. Over time enables: "is my team healthier this month than last month?" and "delegation health trending up or down?"
- **Queryable:** "what keeps coming up in retros?", "what patterns across 1:1s?", "team health trend this quarter"
- Team Health Overview (above) is the point-in-time snapshot. This is the longitudinal record.

### Attention Gap Detection

One-line summary: Surfaces where YOUR attention has dropped — which team members you may be neglecting — based on factual gaps in your own logged data.

- Detects gaps in YOUR behavior, not interpretations of THEIRS:
  - "You haven't logged an observation about Sarah in 52 days"
  - "Your last 3 1:1s with Alex had no new topics added by you"
  - "You haven't given Maya feedback since January"
  - "You have 3 overdue delegations assigned to Marcus that you haven't followed up on"
  - "No career development topics logged for Alex in 4 months"
- All signals are factual date comparisons on structured vault data — zero inference about the other person's state
- Surfaced in daily/weekly notes as nudges, and in Team Health Overview
- **Why this replaced Engagement Signal Detection:** The original feature tried to infer whether a team member "may be disengaged" from 1:1 notes and Slack data. The problem: Myna has YOUR notes, not objective data. "Fewer topics in recent 1:1s" could mean the relationship is healthy and there's less to discuss. "Tone shift in messaging" is unreliable from partial Slack data. Labeling someone as potentially disengaged based on sparse, one-sided data is dangerous — it primes the manager to see problems that may not exist, creating confirmation bias. The flipped version — "where have YOU dropped attention?" — uses the same underlying data (dates of feedback, observations, topics) but the framing is radically different. One judges the employee on incomplete evidence; the other nudges the manager based on their own factual record. False positives are harmless: "you haven't logged about Sarah" might mean you've been giving verbal feedback — it prompts you to log it, not to worry about Sarah.
